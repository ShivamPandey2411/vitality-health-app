# Health Website Planning

## 1. Choose Your Niche
Depending on your expertise and goals, your website could fall into one of these categories:
* **Health & Wellness Blog:** Focuses on lifestyle, nutrition, mental health, or specific fitness routines.
* **Medical Information/Directory:** Providing peer-reviewed data on symptoms, treatments, or a database of local clinics and doctors.
* **Health Tech/SaaS:** A platform for booking appointments, tracking health metrics, or tele-health consultations.
* **E-commerce:** Selling supplements, gym equipment, or wearable health tech.

## 2. Key Features to Include
Regardless of the niche, health websites require specific functionality to be successful:

| Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Search Functionality** | A robust search bar to help users find specific conditions or products. |
| **Mobile Optimization** | Most health-related searches happen on the go. |
| **User Profiles** | Secure areas for users to track their progress or save articles. |
| **Resource Library** | High-quality images, videos, and downloadable guides. |

## 3. The "YMYL" and E-E-A-T Standards
In the world of SEO (Search Engine Optimization), health websites are categorized as **YMYL** (Your Money or Your Life). Google holds these sites to a much higher standard:
* **Experience:** Share personal stories or hands-on involvement.
* **Expertise:** Who is writing the content? (e.g., Doctors, certified trainers, or nutritionists).
* **Authoritativeness:** Are other reputable health sites linking to you?
* **Trustworthiness:** This is the most important. You must cite your sources and keep information up to date.

> [!WARNING]
> **A Note on Compliance:** If you plan on handling patient data or offering medical advice, you must look into HIPAA (in the US), GDPR (in Europe), or local health data regulations to ensure user privacy is protected.
